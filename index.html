Dr Patrick Carden

Summary:

Positive, highly motivated and always ready for new challenges. PhD in ML & statistics of Professional Rugby injury aetiology.

Education:

  • PhD Sport and Health Sciences ‘Exploring the aetiology of high burden lower limb injuries in male professional rugby union players’ - University of Exeter.

    The PhD was co-funded by the Exeter Chiefs Rugby club. The overall aim of the project was to develop algorithms using Python (3) and R statistical software package to quantify the relationship between player motion capture, strength and GPS data and the risk of sustaining lower limb injury in Rugby Union players. I used a variety of data collection equipment including: Force platforms, motion capture, isokinetic dynamometry and a player tracking system incorporating GPS and inertial measurement units. During this project it was necessary to create and maintain a large number of databases. During my time as a PhD researcher, I have been able to foster strong industry partnerships with Codamotion (motion capture manufacturer). I have organised preseason data collection for the Exeter Chiefs Rugby club for the past five years of my PhD. Part of this project required extensive planning, managing and training a team of data collection assistants to ensure the data collection went according to plan and the data collected was of a high standard.
